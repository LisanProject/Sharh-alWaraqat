1 Lecture II: Defining Fiqh and Legal Rulings [11/15/2025, Jumada al-Awwal 24, 1447 AH]

Poem of Imam al-Shafii — Keys to Knowledge

أخي لن تنال العلم إلا بستة
سأنبئك عن مجموعها ببيان

ذكاء وحرص واجتهاد وبلغة
وصحبة أستاذ وطول زمان


My brother, you cannot gain knowledge except with six.
I will inform you of them altogether with explanation.
A sharp mind, passion, effort, and sustenance.
A teacher’s companionship, and lengthy time.

This lesson continues our study of al-Waraqat with Sheikh Abdullahi by clarifying the usuli meaning of legal rulings (ahkam), distinguishing obligation from action, and explaining how rulings relate to the acts of the mukallaf. We focus on wujub (obligation) and ibahah (permissibility) in this lesson with the goal to cover the other categories of ahkam in subsequent lessons.

1.1 What is Usul al-Fiqh?

Usul al-fiqh refers to the principles and methodology by which legal rulings are derived, not the rulings themselves.

Level Meaning
Linguistic “Roots” + “understanding”
Technical Principles governing the derivation of rulings through ijtihad

1.2 What Does Fiqh Mean?

Linguistically:
> Understanding

Technically:
> Knowing the legal rulings of actions derived through ijtihad

Thus:

  • Knowing terms = ma’rifah
  • Knowing how rulings apply = fiqh

Two outwardly identical actions may differ in ruling based on cause (sabab), conditions, and legal qualification.

1.3 Defining Hukm (Legal Rulings)

خطاب الله المتعلق بأفعال المكلفين بالاقتضاء أو التخيير أو الوضع

A hukm is:

  • A divine address, not an action
  • Related to the acts of legally responsible persons
  • Expressed as command, choice, or legal causation

Rulings apply to actions, not abstract information.

1.4 Common Pitfalls

Mistake: Saying ‘Wajib is the hukm’

The hukm is the divine address (ijab). Wajib describes the act required by that address.

Mistake: Mixing act and ruling

An action (fasting, praying, eating) is not the ruling applied to it.

Mistake: Treating usul as study of evidences

Usul concerns methodology; evidences belong to fiqh.

1.5 Ijab, Wujub, and Wajib

Obligation (wujub) has three related terms:

Term Meaning
Ijab The divine command creating obligation
Wujub The hukm: the legal state of obligation resulting from ijab
Wajib The act required by that obligation

1.5.1 Example: Fasting Ramadan

Aspect Description
Ijab Allah’s command to fast
Wujub Obligation established upon the mukallaf
Wajib The act of fasting
Incorrect Statement

“Fasting is the hukm” or “fasting is wajib” (as a hukm).

Correct Statement

The hukm is wujub established by ijab; fasting is the wajib act that fulfills that obligation.

1.5.2 Additional Examples

Scenario Ijab Wujub Wajib
Fajr Command to pray Obligation established Two raka’ahs of Fajr
Zakah Command + nisab met Obligation upon wealth Paying due amount
Jumuah Command + conditions Obligation established Attending Jumuah prayer
Incorrect Statement

“Fajr itself is wujub” or “Fajr is the hukm.”

Correct Statement

The hukm is wujub; Fajr is the wajib act that fulfills that obligation.

1.6 Ibahah and Mubah

These terms relate to permissibility, not obligation.

Term Meaning
Ibahah The divine allowance granting choice (this is the hukm)
Ibahah The legal effect produced by that allowance
Mubah The action permitted under that ruling
Foundational Principle

الاصل في الاشياء الاباحة
“The default ruling of things is permissibility.”

1.6.1 Examples of Mubah

Action Why It Is Mubah
Choosing between lawful foods No command specifying type
Clothing styles within modesty Neutral in itself
Traveling for leisure Neither commanded nor prohibited
Working a lawful job Neutral act, even if earning provision may become obligatory
Incorrect Statement

“Eating food is mubah.”

Correct Statement

“Ibahah is the hukm; eating food is the mubah action.”

1.7 Summary Table — Obligation vs Permission

Category Hukm (Ruling State) Act
Obligation Wujub (from ijab) Wajib
Permission Ibahah Mubah
